# Operators in JavaScript

Operators are used to perform operations on variables and values.

## Types of Operators

### 1. Arithmetic Operators
- `+` Addition
- `-` Subtraction
- `*` Multiplication
- `/` Division
- `%` Modulus (remainder)
- `**` Exponentiation
- `++` Increment
- `--` Decrement

### 2. Assignment Operators
- `=` Assignment
- `+=` Add and assign
- `-=` Subtract and assign
- `*=` Multiply and assign
- `/=` Divide and assign
- `%=` Modulus and assign

### 3. Comparison Operators
- `==` Equal to (loose equality)
- `===` Strict equal to (type + value)
- `!=` Not equal to
- `!==` Strict not equal to
- `>` Greater than
- `<` Less than
- `>=` Greater than or equal to
- `<=` Less than or equal to

### 4. Logical Operators
- `&&` AND
- `||` OR
- `!` NOT

### 5. String Operators
- `+` Concatenation
- `+=` Concatenation assignment

### 6. Ternary Operator
- `condition ? valueIfTrue : valueIfFalse`
